# First-round indexing sources

Where seed firms and projects come from, in priority order. Every record cites
its specific sources; this is the survey list for building coverage.

## Trade and professional organizations

| Source | Covers | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **SEGD** (Society for Experiential Graphic Design) — segd.org | Exhibit design, environmental graphics, experience design firms | Member/firm listings; **SEGD Global Design Awards** archives are project-level records with credits |
| **AAM** (American Alliance of Museums) — aam-us.org | Museums and their vendors | **MUSE Awards** (Media & Technology) archives; annual meeting exhibitor lists |
| **GACEP** (Global Association of Customer Engagement Professionals, formerly ABPM) — gacep.com | Executive briefing centers, customer engagement centers | The corporate-experiential half: recognized programs, member companies, vendor ecosystem |
| **MCN** (Museum Computer Network) | Museum technology | Conference programs name firms + projects |
| **TEA** (Themed Entertainment Association) | Attractions, immersive venues | **Thea Awards** archives (project-level, credited) |
| **AIA** (American Institute of Architects) | Architecture of record | Award archives for museum/cultural buildings — the architecture layer of credits |

## Publications and press

| Source | Use |
|---|---|
| blooloop.com | Project announcements and firm news, museum + attractions |
| Exhibition (NAME journal) / Museum magazine (AAM) | Project write-ups with credits |
| MuseWeb / Museums and the Web papers | Named projects + firms, deep technical credits |
| Institutional press releases | Usually the most complete official credit lists |
| Local/architecture press (ArchDaily, Dezeen, Architect's Newspaper) | New-building projects; architecture + exhibit credits |

## Firm-published

- Firm portfolios and case studies (primary source for a firm's own roles;
  cross-check credits against institutional sources where possible)
- Firm press pages and project announcements

## Method notes

- Award archives are the highest-yield project-level source: dated, credited,
  already curated for significance. Work backward year by year.
- Institutional announcements outrank firm portfolios when credits conflict;
  conflicts that can't be resolved are flagged in the record rather than
  silently chosen.
- **A source must be public and independently checkable — a page anyone can
  go look at.** A firm's own published portfolio counts (see
  `docs/editorial-policy.md` § "What counts as a source"). A page that
  returns an error for every reader, a paywalled report, or a claim that
  only exists as something told to the researcher privately does not count
  — even if that's where the lead came from. If the only way to confirm a
  fact is a private/unpublished channel, either find a public page that
  says the same thing or drop the fact.
- **First-person practitioner accounts count** (editorial ruling
  2026-07-19): a named team member's public portfolio page or project
  write-up is a valid source for what that person's firm delivered on the
  project. **Inferential attribution does not**: a credit line whose scope
  merely *covers* a specific capability (e.g. "Interactives, Media") is not
  sufficient evidence that the firm delivered that capability — capability-
  specific claims (such as credit-level technology tags) need a source that
  names the work itself.
