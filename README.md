# The Experiential Design Index

The public record of who built what in experiential design.

The Experiential Design Index catalogs the firms and projects behind museums,
science centers, visitor centers, brand experience centers, executive briefing
centers, and other designed experiences — and credits the full delivery stack
on every project: architecture, exhibit design, media design and software,
AV integration, fabrication, and more.

The industry's work is documented today only in scattered portfolios, award
pages, and press articles. This index makes it a single, structured, openly
licensed record.

## What's in the index

- **Firm records** — what a firm does (its roles in the delivery stack), where
  it's based, its notable projects, and whether it is currently active.
- **Project records** — venue, location, year, project type (from ground-up
  new buildings down to single-gallery exhibits), and full credits: every firm
  involved and the role it played.
- **Venue records** — the museums, centers, and attractions where the work
  lives.

**Current scope:** United States–based firms, first pass. International
expansion is planned.

## Data

Canonical data lives in [`data/`](data/) as YAML — one file per firm, project,
and venue. The schema is documented in [`schema/`](schema/). The published
website is generated from this data.

The data is licensed [CC BY 4.0](LICENSE.md): use it, build on it, cite it —
with attribution to The Experiential Design Index.

## Editorial policy

Inclusion criteria, the firm activity-status methodology, and the correction
process are documented in [`docs/editorial-policy.md`](docs/editorial-policy.md).
In short: firms with at least three projects in the space are included;
activity status is evidence-based and dated; anyone can submit a correction.

## Corrections and additions

Spotted an error, a missing credit, or a firm we should cover? Open an issue
in this repository. Corrections from the firms and institutions involved are
especially welcome and are prioritized.

## Who maintains this

The Experiential Design Index is published and maintained by
[Sitara Systems](https://sitara.systems), a spatial interaction design and
software studio. Sitara Systems is itself listed in the index; see the
[editorial policy](docs/editorial-policy.md) for how self-inclusion is
disclosed and handled.
